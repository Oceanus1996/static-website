HISTORY

The history of Germany national football team.

  • History of the German National Football Team

    The Germany national football team was founded in 1900 and is one of the oldest national teams in the world. In the 1920 Antwerp Olympic football tournament, the German national team participated in the Olympic football tournament for the first time but did not win a medal.

    At the time, the German national team was coached by Heinrich Schmidt, and the players included Friedrich Rugge, Klaus Fischer, Robert Fedden, and George Walker, among others. Although the German national team did not win a medal at that time, they gradually rose in the subsequent matches and became one of the most successful football national teams in the world.

    1920

  • 1934
    First World Cup Medal

    In 1934, Germany played in the World Cup for the first time and won its first World Cup medal by beating Austria 3-2 in the third-place final. The match was dubbed the "Battle of Rome" because it was played at the Olympic Stadium in Rome, the capital of Italy. The head coach of the German team is Otto Nell, one of the pioneers of German football, who founded the German Football Coaching Academy in 1930 and trained many excellent coaches and players.

  • First World Cup title

    In 1954, Germany won their first World Cup title by defeating the mighty Hungarian team 3-2 in the "Miracle Bern". The match is considered one of the greatest matches in the history of football and one of the most important moments in German history. The German team showed tenacious fighting spirit and team spirit during the competition, allowing the Germans in post-war reconstruction to regain their confidence and pride. The head coach of the German team is Sepp Herberg, a legend in German football who led the German team to four World Cups and finished fourth and runner-up in 1958 and 1966 respectively.

    1954

  • 1974
    Second World Cup title

    In 1974, Germany won their second World Cup title by beating the Netherlands 2-1 at home. The match was dubbed the "class of football" as the Dutch team demonstrated their "all-attack, all-defense" tactics, while the German team won with tenacity and efficiency. The core player of the German team is Franz Beckenbauer, the title of "Emperor of Football", one of the greatest players in the world, with great technique, speed, power and leadership. He also led Germany to European Championship titles and runners-up in 1972 and 1976.

  • Third World Cup title

    In 1990, Germany beat Argentina 1-0 in Italy to win their third World Cup title. It was the last tournament before German reunification in which West Germany represented Germany. The match was also a battle of revenge between the two powerhouses, as Argentina won the World Cup four years ago by beating West Germany 3-2 in Mexico

    1990

  • 2006
    World Cup at Home

    In 2006, Germany finished third at the World Cup hosted at home, beating Portugal 3-1. This World Cup has been called the "summer fairy tale" because the German team has won the respect and love of the whole world for its positive offensive football and unity spirit. Germany's head coach is Jürgen Klinsmann, a legend of German football striker who helped Germany win the 1990 World Cup and captained the 1996 European Championship. After he took over the German team in 2004, he underwent a series of reforms, introducing new tactical concepts and young players, and injected new vitality into German football.

  • The Young Squad

    In 2010, Germany showed a young and talented squad at the World Cup, beating Australia, England and Argentina 4-0, but losing 1-0 to Spain in the semifinals. They ended up finishing third, beating Uruguay 3-2. The head coach of the German team was Joachim Loew, Klinsmann's assistant and successor, who continued Klinsmann's reform direction and strengthened the team's technical and controlling capabilities. The centerpiece of the German team is Messette Ozil, an excellent attacking midfielder with superb passing, dribbling and creativity.

    2010

  • 2014
    The Champion

    Germany made history at the 2014 World Cup in Brazil, beating Argentina 1-0 to win their fourth World Cup title. It was their first World Cup win in South America and the first time they won the World Cup as a united Germany.
